An unpaired t-test is used when there are 2 conditions are compared and they are unpaired (i.e., between-groups factor). Unpaired t-test can be used only when the distributions are normal and homogeneity of variances is met (i.e., the distributions have equal variances).

Conditions:

  • Between-subjects experimental design
  • The number of Independent Variable is one
  • The number of levels in Independent Variable are two
  • The dependent variable is in interval scale
  • The dependent variable is normally-distributed in each condition
  • Homogeneity of variances (i.e., variances approximately equal across groups)

Examples:
